Akara Capital Pvt Ltd ISIN INE08XP07431 Face ₹10,000 12% p.a. monthly Matures 11-Jun-2027 Rating BBB

The idea in one line

When a user sells, Aspero buys the bond back and pays the present value today of every future payment it will collect — each future coupon and the principal, shrunk to what it is worth today, then added up. The rate used to shrink them (the buyback rate) is explained next; the specific numbers for this bond appear once that logic is set.

The price is anchored to the settlement date, not the placement date. On screen the quote is indicative — PV'd to the projected settlement date (T+1, or T+2 if placed after the 3 PM cutoff / on a holiday). The final price is struck on the actual settlement date.

How the buyback (discount) rate is set

The sell price discounts future cashflows at a buyback rate. That rate is the user's own purchase YTM plus a small spread that compensates Aspero for warehousing risk. The spread is banded on the purchase YTM, the bond's residual tenure at purchase, and how long the user has held.

Buyback rate (r) = Purchase YTM + spread  — spread from the tier tables below

Drill-down A — attributes → coupon amounts

Because day-count is Actual/365, each monthly coupon is sized by the number of days in that month — so the coupons are not equal.

Coupon = Face × Coupon% × (days in month ÷ 365) = 10,000 × 12% × (days ÷ 365)
Month lengthCalculationCoupon
31-day month10,000 × 12% × 31/365₹101.92
30-day month10,000 × 12% × 30/365₹98.63
28-day (February)10,000 × 12% × 28/365₹92.05

This is why a single "average coupon" shortcut can't be used — every payment must be valued on its own.

Drill-down B — present value of each payment

From monthly frequency + maturity we get the full schedule (11th of each month, principal on 11-Jun-2027, record date 15 days before each). Each payment is then discounted back to the settlement date at the buyback rate.

PV of one payment = Amount ÷ (1 + r)^(days ÷ 365)  — r = 16.05%, days = settlement → pay date, Actual/365 Sell Price / unit = Σ PV  over every payment Aspero receives (settlement < record date)

Drill-down C — the same build under different scenarios

Change the settlement date and two things move: how many payments Aspero still receives (the record-date filter) and how hard each is discounted. Note the record-date pair — one day flips a whole coupon.

The record-date scenario: the 11-Sep-2026 coupon (₹101.92) has record date 27-Aug-2026. Settling 25-Aug (before it) keeps that coupon in Aspero's price → 11 payments, ₹9,824.12. Settling 28-Aug (on/after it) means the user keeps that coupon → it drops out → 10 payments, ₹9,734.81. The user isn't worse off — they receive the ₹101.92 coupon directly instead. The engine must exclude it, or the coupon is paid twice.

Three rules baked into every price

  1. Discount each dated cashflow individually — coupons are unequal (₹92.05–₹101.92), so no annuity shortcut.
  2. The PV is the dirty price — do not add accrued interest again. It is already inside the discounted next coupon; adding it double-counts.
  3. Record-date filter: include a coupon only if settlement < record date. Otherwise the user is on-record and keeps it.

Reporting & settlement — deriving the anchor date

Placement time vs the 3:00 PM cutoff decides the settlement date, which drives the whole PV.

Order placedReportedSettlement
Working day, before 3:00 PMSame day (by 5 PM)T+1 — next working day
Working day after 3:00 PM, or holidayNext working dayT+1 from reporting ≈ T+2 from placement

"T+1" means the next working day, so weekends and exchange holidays push settlement out further.
