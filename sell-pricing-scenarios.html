1How the sell price is built

The sell price is the present value, on the settlement date, of every future cashflow Aspero will collect after buying the units back — discounted at the buyback rate.

Sell Price / unit =  Σ [ CFₜ × 1 / (1 + r)^(daysₜ / 365) ]  — for every cashflow Aspero receives r    = buyback rate = Purchase YTM + spread = 15.30% + 0.75% = 16.05% daysₜ = calendar days from settlement date to each pay date  (Actual/365) CFₜ  = each remaining coupon (₹92.05–₹101.92) + ₹10,000 principal at maturity

The price the user sees is anchored to the settlement date — not the placement date. When the user is on the screen the bond hasn't settled yet, so the quote is indicative: it is PV'd to the projected settlement date (T+1, or T+2 if placed after cutoff / on a holiday). The final executed price is struck on the actual settlement date.

2Three rules baked into every price

  1. Discount each dated cashflow individually. Coupons are unequal (₹92.05–₹101.92) because of Actual/365 — the annuity shortcut doesn't apply.
  2. The PV is the dirty price — do not add accrued interest again. Accrued interest is already inside the discounted next coupon. Adding it on top double-counts. (Accrued is shown below as info only.)
  3. Record-date filter. Record date = 15 calendar days before each pay date. If settlement ≥ record date, the user is on-record and keeps that coupon, so it is removed from Aspero's PV. Miss this and the user is paid twice.

3Reporting & settlement — deriving the anchor date

Placement time relative to the 3:00 PM market cutoff decides the settlement date, which then drives the whole PV.

Order placedReported to exchangeSettlement
Working day, before 3:00 PMSame day (by 5 PM)T+1 — next working day
Working day after 3:00 PM, or holiday / non-working dayNext working dayT+1 from reporting ≈ T+2 from placement

"T+1" means the next working day, so weekends and exchange holidays push settlement out further.
