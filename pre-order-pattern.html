Key Insights
80% of journeys are dark — fix KycStatusCard first
Only ~20% of first-payment journeys go through tracked touchpoints. KycStatusCard on homepage (uninstrumented) is estimated to drive 50–60% of all first payments. Adding HOME_PAGE_KYC_STATUS_CARD_CLICK is the single highest-ROI instrumentation change.
Bond detail → Invest tap is the universal drop-off
Across every path, only 8–11% of bond detail viewers tap Invest. This is consistent regardless of entry — homepage tile, Explore, or Issuer. The bond detail page is where the buying decision is made (and lost). It's the highest-leverage optimization surface.
Homepage tiles convert 2× better than Explore
High Yield (9.7%) and Short Tenure (10.5%) tiles convert at 2× the baseline (5.1%). Reason: tile taps are already intent-signaling — the user chose a category before landing on the bond. Explore browsers are still deciding.
Issuer path = highest invest-tap rate (14.6%)
Users who navigate via issuer have the strongest conviction before reaching bond detail. They came looking for a specific issuer. 14.6% tap Invest vs 8.2% baseline. Low volume (466 users) but highest quality signal — worth amplifying.
WAYLF path users are most committed once they tap
WAYLF → Explore path: 84.9% tap-to-pay vs 61.6% baseline. Once a user has browsed Explore and tapped Invest, they follow through. The problem is the 10% tap rate — 90% of WAYLF-path bond viewers still don't tap.
Explore → Bond Detail conversion is 55.5% — room to improve
Only 55.5% of Explore viewers tap a bond to view its detail. Homepage tile clickers go to bond detail at 94–96% (direct link). Explore requires the user to browse and self-select. Better recommendations and bond sorting on Explore would improve this step.
Caveats: All funnel counts = unique users (Amplitude Funnel API, ordered sequences). · BOND_DETAILS_PAGE_VIEW was broken Mar 11–May 3 2026 — only May 4–18 data used (15 days). · Payers counted across paths are NOT mutually exclusive — one user may appear in multiple path funnels if they visited multiple entry points. · "Dark" 80% estimate from KYC Touchpoint analysis (May 7–13 2026), not directly measured in this window. · Short Tenure 100% tap-to-pay is a small-sample artifact (21 users only).
