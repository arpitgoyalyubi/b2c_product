Key Insights
92% of viewers never invest
6,598 users read bond pages and leave without tapping Invest. The bottleneck is browse-to-tap, not tap-to-pay (60.7% complete once they tap).
Heavy research product
Payers viewed 16.2 bond pages and opened the app 37 times before investing. Users are comparing extensively — this is a high-consideration purchase, not impulse.
Android users research more
Android payers: 12 bond views + 28.6 app opens. iOS/Web payers: ~10 views + 16–19 opens. Android (lower-income tiers) is more cautious and needs more reassurance before committing.
Once they tap, they pay
60.7% tap-to-pay conversion is strong. Payment UX and checkout flow are not the problem. Focus interventions on getting users to tap Invest, not on reducing payment friction.
iOS/Web decide faster
iOS users: 19.2 app opens vs 28.6 for Android. Higher-income, higher-conviction buyers who trust the product faster. Consistent with 3–4× higher conversion rate vs Android (from Stage 11 data).
Bond pages are research, not buy
With only 0.42–0.61 bond views per app open, most sessions don't include bond research. Users open the app for other reasons, then sometimes browse bonds. Consider surfacing bond recommendations on the home page.
Non-payers churn after KYC
Post-KYC non-payers have just 0.13 home visits and 1.93 bond views in the 15-day window. They almost entirely ghost the app after completing KYC. This is the biggest activation gap — they finished KYC but never came back to invest.
First-time investors research 50% more
1-tx users: 19.7 bond views, 49 home visits. 2+tx users: 13.1 views, 32 visits. First-time investors browse more before their second purchase — they're warm but undecided. A targeted nudge here would drive repeat investment more than acquisition.
