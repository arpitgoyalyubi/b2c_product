Key reads
Meta is the signup driver — FT quality TBD
Meta scaled from 695 installs/wk (W1) to 4,707/wk (W4) — 6.7× — while holding 64–73% install→signup CVR. It's responsible for the overall CVR jump 15.3% → 39.6%. On first trade, W1 shows 1.1% signup→FT (5 of 445) — on par with Google. High signup CVR ≠ high trading intent. W3/W4 Meta cohorts need 4–6 more weeks to confirm FT quality.
OEM channels: high signup CVR, FT unclear
Xiaomi and Oppo contributed 2,608 installs and 1,377 signups in W4 at 52–54% CVR — far better than Organic (8.9%). W1/W2 show near-0 FT for both (too few installs in those weeks to be meaningful). W4 Xiaomi shows 0.3%, Oppo 0.2% — early signal, confirmed in ~4 weeks.
Employee referral: 27.5% signup→FT (outlier)
W1 employee referral: 48 installs → 40 signups (83%) → 11 first trades (27.5% S→FT). That's 20× the rate of Google (1.2%) and Meta (1.1%). Volume is tiny but intent is validated — warm referrals from employees who explain the product before the install. A structured referral programme scaling this channel is worth evaluating.
13,903 installs wasted on junk — zero first trades
Eight networks (NFMobi, S2S Softsys, Leukmobi, Bigporrs, Lumolinka, Mobileadv, Soulaco, Dreamtaleem) delivered 13,903 installs across 4 weeks, 58 signups (0.4% CVR), and zero first trades. These need to be paused or blocked immediately — budget allocated here produces no measurable business outcome.
