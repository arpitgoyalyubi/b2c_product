Why these two numbers tell different stories
−49% Rate (normalised)
The rate per 1,000 PIN views controls for the fact that biometric users now bypass PIN entirely, so total PIN volume is lower. A 49% drop in rate means enrolled users are genuinely remembering their PINs better — or more likely, they rarely need to type it any more so the PIN-users who do appear are a more confident subset.
−22% Absolute Volume
The absolute drop is smaller because the denominator (total PIN views) also fell. This is still real savings: ~43 fewer PIN resets over 8 days × ~3 min customer support time each = ~2 hrs of CS load saved, growing daily as the enrolled base compounds.

Key Takeaways

🎯
Strong adoption out of the gate. 68.3% of users enrolled on first prompt — well above the 50–60% industry baseline for biometric opt-in on a financial app. 29% deferred, meaning re-prompt in 3 days will drive another enrollment wave.
📈
Bio-logins compounding daily. From 1 on launch day to 150 by Apr 27, and accelerating — because users enrolled earlier are returning to use it. This number will continue growing without any additional launches.
⚠️
iOS Face ID failure rate is the key optimization lever. Platform breakdown reveals the problem is concentrated on iOS: 76% OS failure rate on iOS vs 21% on Android, despite iOS having 3.8× fewer users. This is almost certainly Face ID UX (lighting, mask, glasses, accidental cancel) — not a code bug. Action: add a "Look at your phone" pre-prompt for iOS before triggering the Face ID challenge. The 24 backend challenge failures (1.9%) are also worth a backend investigation.
✅
Near-zero PIN fallback. Only 2 users hit the 3-strike limit and fell back to PIN — confirming biometrics rarely forces degraded UX. Users who fail once typically retry and succeed.
📊
Signin funnel unaffected (expected). OTP conv rate held at 63.3% → 61.9% (within noise). Biometrics acts on the PIN screen post-OTP — it reduces friction for returning users, not the top-of-funnel OTP flow. Retention impact will show in D14/D30 re-login frequency over the coming weeks.
🔜
Watch for in week 3–4: (1) Deferred users re-prompted → expect enrollment to cross 2,800+. (2) D14 re-login frequency for biometric-enrolled vs non-enrolled users. (3) OS prompt success rate trend — should improve as users build muscle memory.
