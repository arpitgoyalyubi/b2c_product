Executive Summary

  • The problem we solved: Since Aspero launched, every bond detail page was invisible to Google. Our app was built as a React client-side app — when Google's crawler visited a bond page, it saw a blank spinner, not the bond name, ISIN, or yield. A headless Chrome test in March 2026 confirmed it: at Google's crawl conditions (4× CPU throttle, 1.5 Mbps), the page had exactly 6 readable characters at the 5-second mark — just "Aspero" in the title tag. We were ranking for nothing because Google had nothing to index.
  • The hidden trap we found: Even beyond the CSR problem, a secondary issue compounded it — ISIN, coupon rate, and trustee data were inside a click-triggered modal. Google cannot click. That data was invisible to crawlers regardless of rendering. We discovered this in a manual DOM audit of the Krazybee bond page: 0 occurrences of "INE07HK07783" in rendered HTML. Zero. Every critical data point locked away.
  • 11 SEO tickets shipped in 5 days (Apr 16–21) — all P0 blockers resolved in one focused sprint: server-side rendering, meta titles per bond, JSON-LD FinancialProduct schema, H1/H2/H3 headers, canonical tags, modal→accordion, partner subdomain noindex, sitemap submission (143 bond pages), and 301 redirects from the legacy invest.aspero.in URLs.
  • Early results already visible: Bond page impressions/day up 3× within 7 days (32 → 97 imp/day). Average position improved from 10.8 → 8.9. 13 ISIN queries now appearing in Google Search Console — these were completely absent before. This is early signal; Google's full re-crawl of all 143 pages takes 2–4 weeks.
  • Full impact expected May 12–Jun 2026. Projection: 3,000–6,000 bond page impressions/week (vs ~900/week today) as Google re-indexes all pages with server-rendered content. Issuer aggregator pages (ASP-3592) and SSR internal linking (ASP-3440) are now Live as of Apr 28 — adding a second and third impression surface. Issuer pages alone could account for ~40% of organic bond traffic (Wint Wealth benchmark).
